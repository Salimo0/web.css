Transportation

Mode of Transportation Description Price Range
Subway Extensive underground train system covering Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, the Bronx, and Staten Island. Fast and efficient way to travel throughout the city. $2.75 per ride (single trip) or $33 for a 7-Day Unlimited MetroCard.
Bus Comprehensive bus network complementing the subway system. Covers areas not served by the subway, providing convenient access to various neighborhoods. $2.75 per ride (exact change or MetroCard required) or $33 for a 7-Day Unlimited MetroCard.
Taxis Iconic yellow taxis available throughout the city. Hail one from the street or find them at designated taxi stands. Offers door-to-door service for shorter trips. Metered fares start at $2.50 plus $0.50 per 1/5 mile or per 60 seconds in slow traffic. Additional charges may apply for tolls, surcharges, and tips.
Ride-Sharing Services Services like Uber and Lyft provide on-demand transportation. Book a ride through their mobile apps for convenient and often cost-effective travel options. Prices vary based on distance, time of day, and demand. Estimate ranges from $10-$25 for shorter trips within the city.
Bicycles Citi Bike, the city's bike-sharing program, offers rental bikes for short periods. Ride between docking stations across the city for an eco-friendly and healthy transportation alternative. $3.50 for a single ride, $12 for a 24-hour pass, or $20 for a 3-day pass. Additional fees may apply for rides longer than 30 minutes.
